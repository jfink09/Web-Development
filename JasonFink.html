About Me

My name is Jason Fink and I am a recent graduate from The Pennsylvania State University with a Bachelor of Science in aerospace engineering while matriculating in pre-medicine. I graduated from the Schreyer Honors College with honors in aerospace engineering. I am currently one of the Co-Founders of QuestUni LLC, a software as a service (Saas) company that helps undergraduate students learn more about, and get connected to research opportunities more easily by matching them to labs and projects based on their interests and career goals. I am also a satellite flight operations engineer for the NASA Swift mission at the mission operations center in State College, PA.

    My Personal Interests

    Swing Trading and Options for Income

    I have a personal interest in trading financial securities and derivatives. My two current strategies find an edge in the market through swing trading underlying stocks and selling option premium. I use a technofundamental approach to swing trading involving William O'Neil's CAN SLIM methodolgy and Stan Weinstein's stage analysis. Regardless of the company's fundamentals, the chart needs to follow certain technical analysis and price action. Most of my swing trades take advantage of momentum in the price caused by increasing earnings, revenues, and sales as well as a period of price consolidation with little volume activity indicating little selling. I try finding volatility contraction patterns (VCPs), a pattern discovered by Mark Minervini. These show tightening of the price acion with each dip getting smaller and tighter under a standard pivot. Specific indicators I like to use as potential support advantage resistance levels are the 5EMA, 10EMA, 21EMA, 50SMA, 150SMA, 200SMA, Bollinger Bands, the Webby RSI, Daryl Guppy's Guppy indicator (GMMA), a 50 day rolling average for volume, and Dr. Eric Wish's green dot indicator. I also overlay the SPY chart to determine relative strength in a specific underlying versus the strength of the market. My options strategy involves selling premium by taking advantage of theta decay in 45DTE contracts as well as high implied volatility (IV) environments.
